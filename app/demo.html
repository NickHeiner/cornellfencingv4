Cornell Fencing Association

About Cornell Fencing

The Cornell Fencing program includes both a women’s varsity team and a men’s club team.

The Cornell Women's Varsity Team is ranked 9th in the nation with an overall record of 16-8 to round out a successful 2014-2015 season! The team placed 5th at the Ivy League Championships, with one fencer receiving All-Ivy Honors. After qualifying ten fencers to compete at NCAA Regionals, three went on to represent the Cornell at the 2015 NCAA Fencing Championship held in Columbus, Ohio. The trio's combined efforts led the Big Red to a 17th place finish across 25 universities, as well as All-American honors.The women's team values success both in the salle and throughout the Ithaca community. In addition to their focus on competitive development, the team enjoys giving back to the community through service initiatives, support of other teams at athletic events, and involvement in a wide variety of organizations across campus. .

The Cornell Men’s Fencing Club is ranked as the 2nd best club team in the United States after two consecutive years defending that title at the United States Association of Fencing Clubs Championships. The Men’s team finished in third place at the 2015 Mid Atlantic Collegiate Fencing Association Championships, ranking as the top club team in the conference and beating out varsity programs such as Lafayette, NJIT, Hunter College, Yeshiva University and Haverford College.

The Cornell Fencing Association (CFA), made up of alumni, friends, and family, is an organization with the goal of supporting the fencing program at Cornell through financial assistance, networking opportunities, mentorship, and other means. For more information, please click on the About CFA tab.

About the CFA

The Cornell Fencing Association is made up of alumni, friends, and family of Cornell Fencing. The CFA was established to encourage, promote, and support the fencing program at Cornell University. The CFA assists Cornell University in creating an environment to enhance student athletes’ lives, in accordance with the mission and goals of Cornell, the Athletic Department, and the NCAA through networking, financial support, mentorship, and other means.

CFA was founded on April 2, 2015 by a group of Cornell fencers and alumni, including Christine McIntosh, Beverly Yang, Valerie Derose, Jake Silverman, Matthew Laks, Michael Fotinatos, Matt Herndon, Bryan Mills, and James McCormick, with the help of Coach Iryna Dolgikh, Professor Alexander Colvin as Fencing Advisor, the Athletic Department, and the Athletic Alumni Affairs Department.

Membership

Become a Member:

Membership is open to any friends, family, or alumni of the fencing program. Supporters of Cornell Fencing can be a member for free by signing up here.

Active Members:

Individuals who have contributed to Cornell Fencing or the Cornell Fencing Association during the Cornell Fiscal year in which their contribution is received are active members. The Cornell fiscal year extends from July 1 to June 30. Active members are able to vote in elections for CFA Board members. You must also be an active member to be eligible for a Board position.
